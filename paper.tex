{\bf 
Electroweak parameters at the LHeC}
\end{Large}
\end{center}

\vspace{2cm}

\begin{abstract}
\noindent
The proposed electron-proton collider LHeC is a unique facility 
where electroweak interactions with a very high precision can 
be studied in a yet largely unexplored kinematic regime of 
spacelike momentum transfer. 
%
We exploit simulated inclusive neutral and charged current 
deep-inelastic lepton proton scattering data at center-of-mass 
energies of 1.2 and 1.3\,\TeV with a total integrated luminosity 
of 1\,ab$^{-1}$. The data include scattering with positrons as 
well as with polarized electrons. 
% 
We estimate the uncertainties of Standard Model parameters 
as well as a number of parameters describing physics beyond 
the Standard Model. Our analysis is based on simultanous fits 
of electroweak physics parameters and parton distribution 
functions. 
%
We demonstrate that the $W$-boson mass can be determined with 
an uncertainty down to $\Delta\mW=6\,\MeV$ and the weak mixing 
angle with a precision of about $\Delta \sin^2 \theta_W = 0.00015$. 
%
An unprecedented precision at the sub-percent level is expected 
for the measurement of the weak neutral-current couplings of the 
light-quarks to the $Z$ boson, improving their present precision 
by more than an order of magnitude.
%
The scale dependence of electroweak effects can be studied with 
a high precision over a large range of four-momentum transfer 
squared up to $\Qsq \simeq 500\,000\,\GeVsq$. 
%
LHeC data will complement the determination of electroweak physics 
parameters from other past of ongoing experiments.  
%
\end{abstract}
